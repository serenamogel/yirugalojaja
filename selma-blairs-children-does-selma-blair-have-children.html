    Selma Blair is an American actress and author best known for her roles in Cruel Intentions, Legally Blonde and Hellboy. In 2021, Selma Blair starred in Introducing, Selma Blair, a documentary about her life since being diagnosed with multiple sclerosis. Her autobiography, Mean Baby: A Memoir of Growing Up, was published by Knopf in 2022.

    Selma Blair was born in the Detroit suburb of Southfield, Michigan, the youngest of four daughters of Molly Ann Cooke and Elliot I. Beitner. Her maternal grandfather was the founder of Penn Fruit and she spent most of her childhood in Philadelphia.

    Her parents are both lawyers; her father was a labor arbitrator and active in the American Democratic Party until his death in 2012; her mother died in 2020. Her parents divorced when Selma Blair was 23 years old. She and her sister Elizabeth officially changed their last name to “Blair”; in her memoir, she wrote that, after a concerted effort by her father’s girlfriend to derail her career, she did not speak to her father for 12 years.

    Selma Blair’s father and maternal grandfather were Jewish; Her Scottish grandmother and mother were Anglican, but Selma and her sisters had a Jewish upbringing and Selma officially converted to Judaism in second grade; her Hebrew name is “Bat-Sheva”. Selma Blair attended Hillel Day School in Farmington Hills and Cranbrook Kingswood in Bloomfield Hills. She then spent two years (1990-1992) studying photography at Kalamazoo College.

    At age 21, Selma Blair moved to New York, where she lived with the Salvation Army. Intent on becoming a photographer, she studied at New York University (NYU) and took acting classes at the Stella Adler Conservatory, Column Theater, and Stonestreet Studios. She returned to Michigan, transferred from NYU to the University of Michigan, and in 1994 graduated summa cum laude with a triple major in photography, psychology, and English. She then returned to New York to pursue a career in art.

    In 1990, while at Cranbrook Kingswood, Selma Blair participated in a production of T.S.’s Murder in the Cathedral. She considered it a failure, but her English teacher told her not to give up; This was the first time she thought she could become an actress. In 1993 in New York, an agent discovered her in an acting class and Selma Blair signed a contract. After 75 auditions, Selma Blair landed her first advertising job, doing television commercials for the Virginia Theater.

    Selma Blair landed her first professional role in a 1995 episode of the children’s sitcom The Adventures of Pete & Pete. In 1996, she had her first film role in the comedy The Broccoli Theory and in 1997, she made her first appearance in a mainstream film, the Kevin Kline comedy In & Out. She auditioned six times and stayed on set for several weeks, but most of her scenes were cut from the final version of the film.

    Her first leading role was in the film Strong Island Boys. She then landed the lead role in Amazon’s fantasy drama High in 1997. The series, proposed as the third show set in the Hercules and Xena mythos genre, was not released, but a It was used in the 2000 Xena: Warrior Princess episode “Lifeblood”. She then appeared in several shorts and independent productions, including the award-winning Debutante.

    Selma Blair had her breakthrough opposite Sarah Michelle Gellar and Ryan Phillippe in the 1999 coming-of-age drama Cruel Intentions. It received mixed reviews, with Variety calling “newcomer” Blair “too broad” and “exaggerating the awkwardness of [her role]”. The film grossed $75.9 million worldwide and earned Blair a nomination for an MTV Movie Award for Best Breakthrough Performance and a win for “Best Kiss,” shares with Gellar.

    In 1999, Selma Blair played Zoe Bean in Zoe, Duncan, Jack and Jane, and was nominated for a Teen Choice Award for “TV – Breakthrough Performance”. In 2000, after her performance in the teen comedy Down for You, Selma Blair won a Young Hollywood Award for “Exciting New Face”. In 2000 and 2002, she was included in Vanity Fair’s “Hollywood’s Next Wave of Stars”.

    Selma Blair co-starred with Reese Witherspoon in the 2001 hit comedy Legally Blonde, as a mature, snobby law student; The Hollywood Reporter found her to have a “powerful presence” in her role. The film topped the US box office in its first week of release; the film grossed $96.5 million in North America and $141.7 million worldwide. She then played a student who has an affair with her professor in the 2001 independent drama Storytelling, alongside Leo Fitzpatrick.

    The film premiered at the 2001 Cannes Film Festival; SPLICEDwire cited Blair and Fitzpatrick for their “painfully realistic performances as an emotionally insecure college student and her cerebral palsy dormitory neighbor and lover.” there.” In its review, View Auckland noted: “The acting is excellent, Selma Blair gives a mature performance that suggests better roles await her than those she already has hitherto.”

    Selma Blair then appeared opposite Cameron Diaz and Christina Applegate in the 2002 comedy The Sweetest Thing. Although the film was generally panned, it grossed $68.6 million worldwide. All three actresses appeared on the cover of Rolling Stone magazine and Selma Blair was nominated for a Teen Choice Award in the category of Choice Movie Actress – Comedy.

    From 2005 to 2007, Selma Blair appeared in a series of independent films. In 2008, she reprized her role as Liz Sherman in Hellboy II: The Golden Army, where her character played a larger role than her predecessor. The film received largely positive reviews and became a worldwide commercial success, grossing US$160 million. Blair was nominated for a Scream Award for Best Actress in a Fantasy Film or Television Series.

    In 2012, Selma Blair returned to television as a female lead with the premiere of FX’s Anger Management, starring Charlie Sheen. She starred in 53 episodes as Dr. Kate Wales, a neurotherapist and Sheen’s love interest. Selma Blair left the series in June 2013, while filming the second season, due to a disagreement with Sheen, who fired her via text message.

    In September 2022, Selma Blair became a contestant on season 31 of Dancing With the Stars, but by week 5 of the competition, she left the competition due to her declining health. Selma Blair is known for her avant-garde style and frequent drastic hairstyle changes. She lent her image to the Marc Jacobs-Brian Bowen Smith clothing line and worked with several other fashion designers, including Karen Zambos, Martin Margiela, Isaac Mizrahi, Reinaldo Herrera, and Stella McCartney.
