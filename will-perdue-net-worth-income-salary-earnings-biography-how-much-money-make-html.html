    William Edward Perdue (born August 29, 1965) is a former NBA basketball player who played on four championship teams in his career, three with the Chicago Bulls (1991-1993) and one with the San Antonio Spurs (1999). Perdue currently works as a studio commentator for Comcast SportsNet Chicago during the Chicago Bulls’ pre-game and post-game broadcasts. He attended Merritt Island High School in Merritt Island, Florida. In 1988, he was awarded Southeastern Conference Player of the Year and SEC Male Athlete of the Year while playing basketball at Vanderbilt University. The Chicago Bulls chose him with the 11th overall selection in the 1988 NBA Draft. He won three titles with the Chicago  Bulls between 1991 and 1993, mostly as a backup to centre Bill Cartwright. During the 1994–95 NBA season, he became a regular starter, averaging 8.0 points and 6.7 rebounds per game. However, with the rise of Luc Longley, he became expendable, and the Bulls sold him to the San Antonio Spurs for Dennis Rodman before the start of the following season’s training camp. In August 1999, Perdue returned to the Chicago Bulls as a free agent after winning his fourth NBA championship with the San Antonio Spurs. In 1999–2000, he started 15 of their 67 games, averaging 2.5 points and 3.9 rebounds. Perdue departed Chicago after the 2000 season and signed with the Portland Trail Blazers, averaging 1.3 points, 1.4 rebounds, and 4.5 minutes per game in 13 games. Over the course of his thirteen-year career, he averaged 4.7 points and 4.9 rebounds a game.
