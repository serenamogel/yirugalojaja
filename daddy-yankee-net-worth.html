    Daddy Yankee was born on  3rd February 1977 in San Juan, Puerto Rico, North America.

    Yankee loved playing baseball, but unfortunately, he had to abandon his baseball dream as he was attacked with a stray round of AK-47.

    The incident injured his hip and right thigh. Thereafter, he started rap music which was pretty popular at that time.

    Later, he blended hip-hop, Latin Caribbean music, and reggae rhythms with Spanish rapping and singing. Daddy Yankee often says that the Latin urban performers influenced him.

    Moreover, he is famous for his songs Gasolina, Despacito, Macarena, etc.

    Altogether, he has won more than 82 awards from 270 nominations to date. Daddy Yankee has won five Latin Grammy Awards, two Billboard Music Awards, 14 Billboard Latin Music Awards, and many more.

    Daddy Yankee | Career

    In the early days, Yankee started his musical journey through hip-hop and Latin rhythms and later gained more success by producing Latin hip-hop Songs.

    Similarly, he has earned the title of King of Improvisation and won the Street Jam Reggae Awards for five consecutive years.

    Daddy Yankee abandoned traditional raps and became one of the first artists to perform reggaetón music worldwide.

    His discography profile includes 55 singles, seven studio albums, 72 music videos, two live albums, two DVDs, two mixtape compilations, a compilation album, and a soundtrack.
